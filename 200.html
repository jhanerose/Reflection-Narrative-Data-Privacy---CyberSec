People are using technology more and more as a result of the world becoming more digital and the development of new technologies. 
        People are becoming more aware of the risks associated with cybersecurity and related fields. Locally, the Philippines has 
        acknowledged how crucial it is to build up regulatory frameworks to safeguard its citizens from cyber risks and to secure their 
        personal data in an increasingly digital environment. Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012, 
        and Republic Act No. 10175, also known as the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012, are two notable pieces of legislation that make an 
        effort to solve these pressing challenges. When taken as a whole, they represent a commitment to fostering a secure online environment.

  • Republic Act No. 10175

    Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012

    On September 12, 2012, Republic Act No. 10175 was passed with the goal of resolving the growing cybercrime problem in the Philippines. The law recognizes several forms of cybercrime, such as identity theft, cybersex, computer-related fraud, and online libel. Since these crimes can have disastrous consequences for both the victims and society at large, it is imperative that the government take strong action to combat the increasing reliance on technology in daily life. Online libel restrictions are one of the most contentious aspects of the Cybercrime Prevention Act. The rule's opponents argue that it might be used to stifle dissent and limit freedom of speech, particularly in a setting where social media is a significant public discourse platform.

  • Republic Act No. 10173

    Data Privacy Act of 2012

    passed on August 15, 2012, with the goal of protecting people's personal information in the Philippines. This regulation is particularly crucial at a time when unauthorized data exchanges and data breaches are become increasingly frequent. Strict measures must be taken by controllers and processors of personal information to prevent breaches and unauthorized access, according to the Data Privacy Act. The law established the National Privacy Commission (NPC), whose responsibilities include monitoring compliance, providing guidance, and educating people and organizations about data privacy. In order to guarantee that businesses abide by data privacy laws and that people are aware of their rights, the NPC is essential.

  • Conclusions

    Coexistence of Republic Act No. 10175 and 10173

    While both Republic Acts Nos. 10175 and 10173 address different facets of the digital world, their interactions differ significantly. Data privacy is crucial to preventing cyber hazards since personal data is often obtained and misused in cybercrime. Through the provision of a framework for data protection, the Data Privacy Act mitigates the risks associated with cybercrime. Additionally, both pieces of legislation indicate the Philippine government's recognition of the need for a comprehensive cybersecurity and data privacy plan. As technology develops, the legal frameworks must adapt to address new concerns. These policies must be regularly updated and altered in order to stay up with the quickly growing digital landscape.
